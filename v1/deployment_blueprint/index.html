Module 05 — Pilot Design & Deployment Blueprint

This module specifies a bounded, reversible pilot for the Restoration Visas & National Regenerative Settlement Program (RV-NRSP). The objective is proof of function—not scale—under real administrative, fiscal, and political conditions.

5.1 Pilot Objectives (Explicit & Measurable)

  • Demonstrate safe intake-to-settlement workflow
  • Stabilize participants within 90 days
  • Restore a defined land parcel with high survival rates
  • Produce visible public goods within one budget cycle
  • Generate auditable cost and outcome data

Design rule: success must be legible within 12–18 months.

5.2 Pilot Geography Selection Criteria

Pilot geography is selected for controllability, not symbolism.

  • Public or crown land with clear jurisdiction
  • Existing infrastructure access (roads, utilities)
  • Local governance consent
  • Low land-use conflict risk
  • Demonstrable ecological upside
Avoid politically saturated sites

5.3 Intake Band & Population Envelope

Initial intake must remain deliberately small.

  • Participants: 150–300 individuals (families preferred)
  • Phasing: staggered arrival in 3–4 cohorts
  • Buffer: reserve housing capacity ≥ 20%

Intake is constrained by housing readiness, not visa enthusiasm.

5.4 Arrival & Stabilization (Day 0–90)

  • Government-backed hotel or transitional lodging
  • Guaranteed meals / stipend during orientation
  • Healthcare enrollment and documentation
  • Skills assessment and placement planning

Early stability is the strongest predictor of long-term success.

5.5 Housing & Shelter Deployment

Shelter is treated as infrastructure, not charity.

  • Modular or prefab units with rapid deployment
  • Participant-assisted construction where appropriate
  • Clear transition from temporary to semi-permanent housing
No self-build without safety oversight

5.6 Land Restoration Scope

Restoration targets must be conservative and survivable.

  • Initial land area sized to labor + skills capacity
  • Mixed-species fruit and support trees
  • Water access and soil prep prioritized

Survival rate > planting volume.

5.7 Governance & Command Structure

  • Pilot Director: single accountable authority
  • Interdepartmental cell: immigration, housing, agriculture
  • Independent monitor: ethics, labor, safety

Clear authority prevents paralysis and blame diffusion.

5.8 Budget Envelope (Pilot Order-of-Magnitude)

  • Arrival & stabilization
  • Housing units & infrastructure
  • Training & supervision
  • Land preparation & planting
  • Administration & oversight

Budget is capped; scope adjusts, not vice versa.

5.9 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Housing stability at 6 and 12 months
  • Participant retention and voluntary continuation
  • Tree survival rates after first and second seasons
  • Cost per participant vs baseline alternatives
  • Public trust indicators

5.10 Failure Containment & Exit

A credible pilot includes a graceful shutdown plan.

  • Participant transition pathways protected
  • Assets repurposed for housing or conservation
  • Public reporting of lessons learned

Reversibility preserves political courage.

5.11 Summary for Executive Decision

This pilot is intentionally modest, auditable, and humane. Its value lies in demonstrated function, not immediate scale.

Approval authorizes learning—not irrevocable commitment.
